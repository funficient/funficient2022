Teal & the Future of Work — A Practical FAQ

Short answers, practical steps, and stories to help you explore teal practices in your organisation.

The success of an organization is measured by the well-being of its employees, not just its financial performance.

— Frédéric Laloux

What is a teal organization? ▾

A teal organization is a self-managed company built around trust, purpose, and wholeness rather than hierarchy and control. It allows people to make decisions, organize work, and contribute based on their strengths instead of job titles.

What does a teal organizational structure look like? ▾

Teal organizations use fluid, role-based structures instead of rigid hierarchies. Authority is distributed, and people organize around work rather than reporting lines.

How does teal project management work? ▾

Teal project management is driven by teams, not managers. Work is prioritized through shared purpose, transparent information, and continuous feedback instead of top-down planning.

What does leadership look like in a teal company? ▾

Leadership in a teal organization is not a position but a function. People step into leadership following a coaching approach when their skills, insight, or energy are needed.

How do teal companies hire people? ▾

Teal companies hire based on values, self-motivation, and ability to collaborate rather than only technical skills or credentials. Teams discover interesting people and build longterm relationships rather than post a job ad and wait for applicants (pull vs push).

How does onboarding work in a teal organization? ▾

Onboarding in teal focuses on relationships, culture, and shared understanding, not just procedures and tools.

How are job titles handled in teal organizations? ▾

Teal organizations use flexible roles instead of fixed job titles, allowing people to evolve as their strengths and interests change.

How do teal organizations train people? ▾

Training in teal is continuous and peer-driven, with learning happening through real work, coaching, and reflection.

Who is teal for? ▾

Teal works best for people who value autonomy, responsibility, learning, and meaningful contribution and impact.

Is teal right for my company? ▾

Teal is a good fit for organizations that want adaptability, trust, and long-term resilience rather than strict control. If you're interested in B Corps, teal might be a good fit for you.

How does decision-making work in teal teams? ▾

Decisions are made by the people closest to the work, using advice from others rather than seeking permission from managers.

How do teal organizations manage performance? ▾

Performance is managed through transparency, regular feedback, and shared accountability rather than rankings and annual reviews.

How do teal teams work without managers? ▾

Teams coordinate through clear agreements, shared goals, defined decision making processes, and peer feedback instead of relying on command-and-control management.

What tools do teal organizations use? ▾

Teal organizations use tools that support transparency, collaboration, and self-organization, without relying on technology. Sticky notes or Miro whiteboards for example are more agile and lean than a stack of high-tech solutions.

How do teal companies handle conflict? ▾

Conflict is handled through a defined conflict resolution process which includes direct, respectful dialogue (like non-violent commnunication) and facilitated reflection rather than escalation to authority.

How do teal organizations scale? ▾

Teal organizations scale by creating clear principles and strong culture rather than adding layers of management.

What is self-management in teal companies? ▾

Self-management means people are trusted to organize their own work, make decisions, and take responsibility for outcomes.

How does teal compare to Agile or Scrum? ▾

Agile and Scrum focus on how work is done, mostly only at product delivery, while teal focuses on how people and power are organized across the entire organization.

Can teal work in remote teams? ▾

Yes, teal works especially well in remote teams because it relies on trust, clarity, and self-organization rather than physical oversight.

What does a day in a teal organization look like? ▾

A day in a teal organization is focused on purposeful work, open communication, shared problem-solving, and continuous learning. Clear goals, collaboration, and excellent communication forms the foundation of a teal work-day.
